Gangtok

Gangtok is the capital of the mountainous northern Indian state of Sikkim. Established as a Buddhist pilgrimage site in the 1840s, the city became capital of an independent monarchy after British rule ended, but joined India in 1975.

Kanchenjunga Falls

The most beautiful thing water can do in the arms of nature is falling from a cliff with majestic demeanour. The Kanchenjunga Waterfall in Pelling is one such vision which will imprint in your memory forever, owing to the spectacular sight it provides which is nothing short of a picturesque extravaganza.

Singshore Bridge

The bridge offers a spectacular view of picturesque surroundings. Dotted with gushing waterfalls and crisscrossed by a winding stream, the landscape enthralls one with its beauty and serenity. Apart from the months of July and August.

Rolep Bridge

A tiny picturesque, Virgin village. Ensconced amidst nature, tucked away like a secret. Located at an altitude of 4000ft, and 20 km from Rongli in East Sikkim. It’s a haven on the slopes of rolling Green Himalayan foothills with a view of Rangpo Khola River Valley.

Ravangla Buddha Park

A small tourist town situated at an elevation of 7000 ft in South Sikkim. It lies between Pelling and Gangtok and acts as a starting point for the Trek to Maenam Wildlife Sanctuary, 65 km from Gangtok.
